The Middle Ages 🏰

This period marks the time between the fall of ancient Rome and the start of the modern age.

When? From 476 AD (fall of Rome) to 1492 AD (discovery of America).

Stages:

  • Early Middle Ages (5th–11th centuries)
  • High Middle Ages (11th–13th centuries)
  • Late Middle Ages (13th–15th centuries)

Why did it start?

  • Invasions by barbarian tribes
  • Division of the Roman Empire
  • Economic and political crisis

    The words List ✅

    -Monotheist: person who believes in only one god.

    -Polytheist: person who believes in many gods.

    -Basileus: emperor of the Byzantine Empire. He held absolute power and ruled through his government, the army and the church.

    -Latifundia: large estates of land used for farming.

    -Heretic: person who disagreed with the teachings of the church.

    -Buttres: pillar attached to a wall. It is used to reinforce the strength of the wall.

    -Greek-cross floor plan: church floor plan with a square central area and four arms of equal length.

    -Pilgrimage: journey to a holy place to show religious devotion.

    -Vizier: minister of the caliphate. He was in charge of tax collection and the government.

    -Emir (wali): governor of a province of the caliphate.

    -Mosque: religious temples used by the Muslims. Its structure replicated Muhammad’s house and consisted of a courtyard and a prayer hall.

    -Arrabal: neighbourhood placed outside the wall in a Muslim city where the poor lived.

    -Citadel (alcazaba): fortified palace protected by soldiers in a Muslim city.

    -Souk: market in a Muslim city.

    -Minaret: high tower in a mosque used to call people to prayer.

    -Qibla: wall in a mosque that faces Mecca.

    ⚔️ Age of Invasions

    1️⃣ The Germanic Peoples

    • Lived in rural villages; main activity → farming
    • Only a small minority had power; divided territories to keep control
    • Formed groups across Europe:
      • Ostrogoths → Italy
      • Visigoths → Hispania (Spain)
      • Suevi → Northwest Iberian Peninsula
      • Franks → Gaul (France)
      • Angles, Jutes, Saxons → Britain (early England)

    2️⃣ Other Invaders

    ◆ Slavs

    • Origin: north of the Carpathian Mountains (6th century)
    • Groups:
      • South Slavs → Balkan Peninsula
      • Western Slavs → Central Europe
      • Eastern Slavs → Russia & Ukraine
    • Farmers, builders, warriors

    ◆ Vikings

    • Origin: Denmark, Norway, Sweden
    • Period: 8th century
    • Known for: “Drakkar” long ships
    • Explored & raided Northern Europe
    • Settled: England & France (9th–10th centuries)

    ◆ Magyars

    • Origin: Great Plains
    • Attacked Central & Western Europe
    • Settled in Pannonia → formed Kingdom of Hungary (10th century)

    ✨ The Byzantine Empire

    The Eastern Roman Empire resisted invasions and lasted for a thousand years.

    Political Organization

    • Basileus: emperor with absolute power, ruling through government, army, and church.
    • Justinian’s Code: legal reform based on ancient Roman laws.

    Economy

    • Main activity: agriculture (large estates called latifundia).
    • Craftwork: silk, mosaics, jewelry, dyes, and tapestries.
    • Trade: connected Europe and Asia.

    Society

    • Basileus
    • Landowning clergy and nobility
    • Artisans, traders, soldiers, free farmers
    • Serfs and slaves

    Religion

    • Christianity was the state religion.
    • Iconoclastic Wars: emperors banned icons (8th–9th centuries).
    • East–West Schism: in the 11th century, the Byzantine Church separated from the Catholic Church.

    Byzantine Art

    • Architecture: domes, semicircular arches, Greek-cross floor plans.
    • Decoration: mosaics, golden icons, religious themes.
    • Examples: San Vitale (Ravenna), Saint Mark’s Basilica (Venice).

    🕌 Islam — Empire & Innovation

    🌍 Arabia Before Islam

    • Arabia was inhabited by nomadic tribes living in the desert.
    • Main economic activity: caravan trade with luxury goods.
    • They were polytheists; Mecca was both religious and commercial center.
    • Arabic was the common language.

    🌙 The Birth of Islam

    • Muhammad received revelations from the angel Gabriel (7th century).
    • In 622 AD, he fled Mecca to Medina — the Hegira.
    • He returned to conquer Mecca in 630 AD and united Arabia under Islam.

    🕋 The Muslim Religion

    • Holy Book: The Qur’an.
    • Preacher: The imam, who leads prayer.

    Five Pillars of Islam

    • Belief in one God (Allah) and Muhammad as his prophet.
    • Pray five times a day facing Mecca.
    • Fast during Ramadan until sunset.
    • Pilgrimage to Mecca at least once.
    • Charity to the poor.

    Other Rules

    • No alcohol, gambling, pork, or depictions of Allah or Muhammad.

    ⚖️ Political Development — The Caliphates

    • Orthodox Caliphate (632–661): Capital: Medina; expanded into Egypt, Syria, Persia.
    • Umayyad Caliphate (661–750): Capital: Damascus; expanded into India, Samarkand, Iberian Peninsula.
    • Abbasid Caliphate (750–1258): Capital: Baghdad; Golden Age of Islamic culture; ended after Mongol invasion.

    Expansion succeeded due to weak neighboring empires, religious motivation (Jihad), and tolerance toward Christians and Jews.

    💰 Economy

    • Livestock: goats, sheep, camels, donkeys, horses.
    • Irrigation systems: dykes, waterwheels.
    • New crops: rice, cotton, sugarcane, citrus, aubergines, carrots, spinach.
    • Trade: Controlled major routes; used gold dinar and silver dirham.

    🏙️ Society

    • Classes: Nobility, traders/artisans/farmers, slaves.
    • Religious division: Muslims (citizens) and dhimmi (non-Muslims paying tax).
    • Women: Patriarchal society; equality before Allah; worked mainly at home.

    📚 Cultural Development

    • 🩺 Medicine: Avicenna (Ibn Sina) developed surgery and anesthesia.
    • 🧮 Mathematics: Zero, algebra, Arabic numerals.
    • 🌠 Astronomy: Star catalogues and observatories.
    • 📜 Philosophy: Averroes (Ibn Rushd) spread Aristotle’s ideas.

    🏗️ Architecture & Art

    • Buildings: Mosques, palaces, mausoleums.
    • Materials: Brick and adobe; use of water and light in decoration.
    • Elements: Minaret, courtyard (sahn), fountain (sabil), prayer hall (haram), qibla wall with mihrab and minbar.
    • Decoration: Arabesques, calligraphy, lattice-work, horseshoe and lobed arches.
    • Examples: Great Mosque of Córdoba, Alhambra (Granada), Al-Azhar Mosque (Cairo), Taj Mahal (India).
